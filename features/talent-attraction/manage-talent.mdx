Once your job post is live, the platform automatically matches candidates from its talent pool. This page covers how to review matches, send invitations, and manage applicants through the hiring pipeline.

## Review talent matches

The platform identifies candidates whose skills align with your job requirements. Matches are split into two categories:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Internal matches">
    Existing employees within your organisation who have the skills for the role. Internal matches are displayed with match scores.

    [Insert image 27 here]
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="External matches">
    Candidates from outside the organisation. External match details may require unlocking.

    [Insert image 28 here]

    <Note>
      Viewing full external match profiles may incur a fee (e.g. $50 per 100 matches). Contact your administrator for details on your plan.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Understanding match scores

Each candidate's profile shows a compatibility indicator:

| Indicator | Meaning |
|:---|:---|
| **Strong** | High skill alignment — candidate meets most or all requirements |
| **Medium** | Partial alignment — candidate meets some requirements and may need upskilling |

The **Profile Snapshot** visualises skill alignment using colour-coded indicators:

* 🟢 **Green** — Matched skills
* 🔴 **Red** — Skill gaps
* ⚫ **Grey** — Other skills the candidate has

## Invite talent to apply

When you find a strong match, you can invite them to apply directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a candidate">
    From the matches list, click **Send Invitation** on the candidate's profile card.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customise the invitation">
    A message template will appear. Customise the invitation message to suit the role and candidate.

    [Insert image 29 here]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send">
    Click **Send**. The candidate will receive the invitation and you can track its status (including the timestamp of when it was sent).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Personalised invitation messages tend to have higher response rates. Mention specific skills or experience that caught your attention.
</Tip>

## Review applicants

Manage candidates who have applied to your job post through a structured pipeline.

[Insert image 30 here]

### Pipeline stages

| Stage | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Applied** | Candidate has submitted an application |
| **Reviewed** | Application has been viewed by the hiring team |
| **Shortlisted** | Candidate has been selected for further consideration |
| **Interviewed** | Candidate has been scheduled for or completed an interview |
| **Successful** | Candidate has been selected for the role |
| **Keep in View** | Not selected this time but worth considering for future roles |
| **Rejected** | Candidate has been declined |

To move a candidate between stages, use the status dropdown on their profile card.

<Info>
  You will receive automated notifications if an applicant updates their profile after applying. A visual warning will appear if an applicant's profile becomes deactivated.
</Info>
