</Note>

The Skills Inventory provides a comprehensive list of all the experiences, competencies, and professional skills of current employees — designed for organisational planning and workforce capability analysis.

## All Skills view

When you open the Skills Inventory, you will see a department-level overview of skills across the organisation.

[Upload Image 1 here]

The main table displays:

| Column | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Department Name** | The department within the organisation |
| **# of Employees** | Total number of employees in the department |
| **Expertise** | A visual proficiency distribution indicator showing skill depth across the department |

* Use the **Search** bar to find departments by name or skill keyword
* Use the **Filter** controls to narrow results by specific skills (e.g. "Python")
* Results show how many departments match your filter criteria

---

## Department drill-down

Click on any department row to view the individual employees and their skill details.

[Upload Image 2 here]

The employee table shows:

| Column | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Name** | Employee's full name and email address |
| **Job Title** | Their current role within the organisation |
| **Department** | The department they belong to |
| **Proficiency** | Self-assessed proficiency level (shown as a visual indicator) |
| **Manager's Rating** | The manager's assessment of the employee's proficiency |

* View individual employee skill profiles by clicking on their name
* Use pagination to browse through large teams

---

## Skill search

Use the top-level **Search Skills** bar to find specific skills across the entire organisation.

[Upload Image 3 here]

* Search by skill name to see which departments and employees have the expertise
* View the **Top Skills** filter to quickly access the most prevalent skills
* Results update dynamically as you refine your search

<Tip>
  Use the Skills Inventory to identify subject matter experts across the organisation when staffing new projects or forming cross-functional teams.
</Tip>
