The Individual Development Programme (IDP) is a collaborative tool that helps employees and managers work together on short-term and long-term development goals. It provides a structured framework for setting goals, planning actions, and tracking career growth.

## IDP lifecycle

Your IDP progresses through three stages:

| Stage | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Draft** | You are still working on your plan. You can edit and update your goals at any time. |
| **Submitted** | You have submitted the plan for your manager's review. |
| **Approved** | Your manager has reviewed and approved your development plan. |

---

## Creating your IDP

To create or update your IDP, navigate to **My Profile** and select the **Individual Development Plan** section.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Assessment">
    Start by reflecting on your current strengths and areas for improvement. This section is a space for honest self-assessment and discussion points with your manager.

    [Upload Image 1 here]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Development goals">
    Define your development goals. For each goal, specify:

    [Upload Image 2 here]

    | Field | Description |
    |:---|:---|
    | **Goal category** | Select from available categories (e.g. Technical, Leadership, Communication) |
    | **Goal name** | A specific, descriptive name for your goal |
    | **Priority** | Set as High, Medium, or Low |
    | **Target date** | Choose a deadline for achieving the goal |
    | **Status** | Track progress as you work towards the goal |
    | **Status notes** | Add context or updates on your progress |

    You can add multiple goals by clicking **Add +**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Action plan">
    Create concrete action steps to achieve your development goals. Actions can include:

    [Upload Image 3 here]

    * Enrolling in recommended courses or certifications
    * Participating in gigs or cross-functional projects
    * Seeking mentorship from a colleague
    * Completing specific assignments or deliverables

    Link learning resources directly from the platform to keep your plan actionable.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Self-evaluation — Career interest">
    Complete a self-evaluation of your career interests. This section helps align your development plan with your broader career direction.

    [Upload Image 4 here]

    * **Career interests** — Search and select roles you are interested in
    * **Relocation (short-term)** — Indicate if you are willing to relocate in the short term and specify preferred locations
    * **Relocation (long-term)** — Indicate if you are willing to relocate long-term
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and submit">
    Review all sections of your IDP for completeness, then click **Finish** to submit it to your manager for approval.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Your IDP is a living document. You can revisit and update your goals, action items, and self-evaluation at any time while the plan is in **Draft** status.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Discuss your IDP with your manager during regular check-ins. Collaborative goal-setting leads to more targeted and achievable development outcomes.
</Tip>
