Gigs let you step out of your comfort zone and immerse yourself in different functions across the company. Whether you want to shadow a leader, join a mentorship, or contribute to a cross-functional project, Gigs provide short-term opportunities designed to expand your skills and network.

## Types of gigs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="user-tie" title="Leadership Shadowing">
    Experience senior leadership first-hand. Leadership Shadowing gigs are short-term (up to 2 days) and allow you to observe how leaders approach strategic decision-making, communication, and planning.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="handshake" title="Mentorship">
    Long-term development opportunities lasting 1–6 months. Mentorship gigs pair you with an experienced professional who guides your growth in a specific area.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="diagram-project" title="Project">
    Contribute to organisational projects over 1–6 months. Project gigs typically require 5–15% of your time and let you gain hands-on experience with cross-functional teams.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="binoculars" title="Role Shadowing">
    Observe a team or squad in action. Role Shadowing gigs are short-term (up to 2 days) and give you visibility into how another function operates day-to-day.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

When you open the Gigs module, you will see curated gig recommendations based on your current role, aspired role, skills you want to learn, and popular gigs.

Recommendations are organised into two categories:

<AccordionGroup>

## Apply for a gig

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a gig">
    From the recommendations or browse tabs, click on a gig card to view its details.

    <Frame>
      ![Image](/images/image-30.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the opportunity">
    Check the gig description, activities, time commitment, host details, and available slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply">
    Click **Apply** and select your preferred time slot. Submit your application for the host to review.

    <Frame>
      ![Image](/images/image-31.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Provide feedback

After completing a gig, you will be prompted to provide feedback on your experience.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-33.png)
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm participation">
    Confirm whether you were able to participate in the opportunity on your applied slot date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share your feedback">
    If you could not attend, select the reason (e.g. date rescheduled, unable to contact host, personal commitments) and add any comments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit** to complete your feedback.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating a gig (for hosts)

<Note>
  This section is for users with a **TM Gig Creator** role.
</Note>

As a gig host, you can create opportunities for others to learn from you or your team.

### Create an opportunity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select gig type">
    From the Gigs page, click **Create an opportunity** and choose from Leadership Shadowing, Mentorship, Project, or Role Shadowing.

    <Frame>
      ![Image](/images/image-34.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define the purpose">
    Fill in the opportunity details:

    <Frame>
      ![Image](/images/image-35.png)
    </Frame>
    | Field                      | Description                                                                           |
    | :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
    | **Title**                  | Describe the opportunity's purpose in one sentence (minimum 10 characters)            |
    | **About this opportunity** | Provide a detailed description of the exposure and activities participants can expect |
    | **Learning objectives**    | Outline what participants can expect to learn                                         |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Input gig details">
    Configure the timeframe, number of participants, commitment level, mode of communication (e.g. Physical, Virtual, or Mixed Session), and host details.

    <Frame>
      ![Image](/images/image-36.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

### Gigs you're hosting

As a gig host, you can view all your created gigs under the Gigs you're hosting tab.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-37.png)
</Frame>

- View gigs categorised by type (e.g. Project, Role Shadowing)
- See the number of awaiting responses and applicants
- Click **Review applicants** to manage submissions

### Opportunity details

Click on any gig to view its full details, including:

- Opportunity description and learning objectives
- Host details and available slots
- Tabs for **Awaiting Review** and **Scheduled Sessions**

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-38.png)
</Frame>

You can also **Edit post**, **Duplicate**, or **Unpublish** the gig from this view.

<Info>
  If an opportunity has ended, a short notice will be displayed and editing will be restricted.
</Info>

---

### Review applicants (host view)

From the **Awaiting Review** tab, review each applicant's profile:

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-39.png)
</Frame>

- View the applicant's **Match Score** and **Skills Profile**
- See why they are interested in the opportunity
- **Shortlist** or **Decline** each applicant

### Provide feedback (host view)

After a gig session is completed, hosts can provide feedback for each participant from the **Provide Feedback** tab.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-40.png)
</Frame>

- View accepted attendees grouped by slot
- Click **Provide Feedback** or **Edit Feedback** for each participant
