The Team & Members module gives managers a bird's-eye view of their team's skills, strengths, and development areas. From here, you can explore individual profiles, identify skill gaps, and send targeted recommendations to support your team's growth.

## Team dashboard

When you open Team & Members, you will see a high-level summary of your team's capabilities.

[Upload Image 1 here]

The dashboard includes:

* **Top 5 Skills** — The most common skills across your team
* **Team Skills Index** — A visual indicator of your team's overall skill health
* **Top 5 Aspiring Roles** — Roles your team members are most interested in pursuing

---

## Member profiles

Click on any team member to view their individual talent profile.

[Upload Image 2 here]

Each profile shows:

* **Career bio** — A summary of the member's professional background
* **Current role** and department
* **Skills** — Listed with proficiency bars showing their self-assessed levels
* **Aspirations** — The roles they are working towards

---

## Send recommendations

As a manager, you can recommend specific skills or gig opportunities to your team members.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Send Recommendations">
    From the Team & Members page, click **Send Recommendations**.

    [Upload Image 3 here]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select skills to recommend">
    Search and select the skills you want to recommend. For each skill, you can:

    * Add a personalised note explaining why you think the skill is relevant
    * Attach recommended learning opportunities or gig opportunities

    [Upload Image 4 here]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose recipients">
    Select which team members will receive the recommendations. You can choose one or more members from your team.

    [Upload Image 5 here]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and submit">
    Review all the information and click **Submit**. The selected team members will be notified by email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When recommending skills, include a note explaining the context — for example, how the skill connects to an upcoming project or the team member's career aspirations.
</Tip>
