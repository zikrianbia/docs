To get the most out of the platform, it's important to understand how we categorize and measure skills.

## Skill types

We differentiate between skills based on their relevance to your selected role.

### Core skills
These are the essential skills required for a specific role. They are the "must-haves" that employers and the platform look for first. High proficiency in core skills significantly boosts your match score.

### Non-core skills
These are supplementary skills that add value but are not strictly required for the role. They can include soft skills, adjacent technical skills, or legacy experience.

## Measuring proficiency

Each skill in your profile is assigned a proficiency level and a CSP score.

### Proficiency levels
We use a standard scale to measure how well you know a skill:
* **Novice**: Basic understanding, requires significant guidance.
* **Intermediate**: Can perform tasks independently but may need help with complex issues.
* **Advanced**: High level of competency, can lead projects and mentor others.
* **Expert**: Deep authority, recognized expert in the field.

### CSP (Critical Skill Points)
Critical Skill Points (CSP) represent the "weight" or importance of a skill in the current job market. 
* A **Higher CSP** means the skill is in high demand and is a significant differentiator for your role.
* CSP scores are updated dynamically based on real-time market data.

<Info>
  Your overall profile strength is a combination of your **Proficiency Level** and the **CSP** of the skills you possess.
</Info>
